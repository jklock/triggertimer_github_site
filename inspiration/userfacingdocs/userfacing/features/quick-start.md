# Quick Start Guide

Get started with TriggerTimer in under five minutes.

---

## What You Need

- **iPhone** running iOS 26 or later
- **Apple Watch** (optional, for hands-free shot detection)
- **SG Timer** (optional, for professional-grade timing)

---

## Step 1: Launch the App

Open TriggerTimer. You'll see five tabs at the bottom:

- **Home** — Dashboard with quick actions and recent sessions
- **Drills** — Browse and start training drills
- **Sessions** — View your training history
- **Data** — Analyze performance trends and graphs
- **Settings** — Configure devices, sync, and preferences

---

## Step 2: Start Your First Session

### Quick Session (Fastest Way)

1. Tap the **Home** tab
2. Tap **Start Quick Session**
3. The timer will begin a countdown
4. Dry-fire when ready
5. Your shot time is recorded
6. Tap **Stop** when finished

### From a Drill Template

1. Tap the **Drills** tab
2. Browse categories: Draw, Reload, Transition, Speed, etc.
3. Tap a drill to see details
4. Tap **Start Drill**
5. Follow the on-screen instructions

---

## Step 3: Review Your Results

After ending a session:

1. Tap the **Sessions** tab
2. Tap your recent session
3. View:
   - Total time
   - Split times per shot
   - Performance graph
   - Drill breakdown

---

## Step 4: Connect Your Devices (Optional)

### Apple Watch

1. Install TriggerTimer on your Apple Watch
2. Open the Watch app
3. Tap **Start Training**
4. The Watch detects shots via its microphone

### SG Timer

1. Go to **Settings** → **Devices** → **SG GO**
2. Turn on your SG Timer
3. Tap to connect when it appears
4. The timer will now be your primary timing source

---

## Next Steps

- **Customize Drills** — Create your own training templates
- **Track Progress** — Use the Data tab to see trends over time
- **Export Data** — Share sessions as CSV, JSON, or PractiScore format

---

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Quiet environment** — Reduces false detections
2. **Consistent grip** — Improves split time accuracy
3. **Regular training** — Short daily sessions beat long weekly ones
4. **Review your data** — Patterns reveal improvement opportunities
